Mood Diary

Mood diary is a free mood tracker for android. It's made for my personal needs and has been rapidly developed, using some ideas I thought up years ago.

The idea comes from a paper-submission form I've used, which had fields for writing down certain things such as mood in a scale from 'very depressed' to 'very elevated', anxiety level, and a few more.

The paper mood diary

I soon switched to a similar system, but using textfiles instead of paper. I didn't need much more; the important part was the tracking.

ASCII version

I wrote some elaborate python scripts to parse the ASCII file into something more practical (i.e JSON), but I didn't spend much time on making fancy-looking graphs. Eventually I forgot about updating the files.

Javascript-based graph hack

Now I've fallen far behind on the logging because of the effort in adding an entry, or not being near a computer with the files. Lame. So, here it is. My attempted solution to this problem. The app itself is pretty simple: add a few measurements you want to note down, and a reminder or two. At the specified time, Mood Diary will appear, asking you to submit the measurements. When it's time to interpret the data, export the entire database, or make a custom CSV which you can import to your favourite spreadsheet or plotting software. Perhaps future versions (or forks) will have an integrated chart.
